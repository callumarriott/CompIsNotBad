    Computer Scientists have done some incredible things!

    Here are some famous examples:

    • Alan Turing - The Cryptographer
    • Grace Hopper - The Admiral
    • Tim Berners-Lee - The Networker
    • Ada Lovelace - The Mathematician
    • Steve Wozniak - The Mastermind
    • Edsger W. Dijkstra - The Travelling Salesman
      • Now to go into some more detail,

      1. Alan Turing
      Alan Turing was one of the original inventors of modern computers. Originally educated as a mathematician, he went on to help the British Intelligence Service develop several predecessors to modern computers, with the aim of decrypting enemy intelligence during WW2, thus making him arguably the most influential figure in the field of cryptography. Unfortunately despite his astonishing contributions, the British Government at the time thanked him by prosecuting him for homosexual activity and chemically castrating him, leading to his tragic suicide.
      2. Grace Hopper
      Rear Admiral Grace M. Hopper entered the field of Computer Science near the end of WW2, and pioneered some of Computer Scientists most important concepts, and popularized the now familliar idea of programming languages independent from the machine they were written for, in fact she was the creator of of COBOL, one of the first high-level languages, and the only from that time still in usage today.
      3.Tim Berners-Lee
      Tim Berners-Lee has done many things throughout his life, but definitely his most famous, and most influential, was the invention of the World Wide Web, proposing the initial idea early 1989, and creating a working prototype within the year. Today he is still active within the field, and is currently campaigning to make major changes to the way in which the internet currently exists.
      4. Ada Lovelace
      Ada Lovelace was not a Computer Scientist in traditional terms, given that she died halfway through the 1800's she had little opportunity to interact with computers as we would recognise them today, however it would be difficult to argue against her being one of the most influencial early figures in the field. A mathematician at her core, Ada Lovelace is often considered to be the inventor of the algorithm, a concept vitally integrall to how computers work today.  
      5. Steve Wozniak
      Co-founder of Apple back in 1976, Wozniak is often considered to be the real genius behind the company, which was responsible for creating some of the earliest computers designed for every day use rather than as exclusively specialist equipment. He designed all of the products released by the company over the first decade. Since then he has worked in a number of areas within the field, and today works in data visualisation.
      6. Edsger W. Dijkstra
      One of the most important contributors to theoretical computer science, Dijkstra has created a number of useful and interesting theories and algorithms, most famously being the author of Dijkstra's Algorithm, a solution to the Travelling Salesman problem that involves finding the shortest route around a weighted network.
